Social and Emotional Development

Language and Literacy

Speaking and Listening:

Encouraging verbal communication to express thoughts, needs, and ideas. Building conversational skills, including turn-taking, asking questions, and storytelling. Developing listening skills to understand instructions, stories, and conversations.

Vocabulary Development:

Introducing new words daily through conversations, books, and activities. Expanding a child’s ability to recognize and understand a variety of words for effective communication.

Phonological Awareness:

Teaching children to recognize and manipulate sounds in words, such as rhyming or identifying syllables. Strengthening the ability to recognize letters and connect them to the sounds they make.

Reading:

Introducing age-appropriate books to foster a love for reading. Guiding children in identifying letters, words, and eventually full sentences. Encouraging comprehension by discussing stories, predicting outcomes, and asking questions about the plot or characters.

Writing:

Encouraging mark-making and scribbling as precursors to writing. Supporting the development of fine motor skills to form letters and numbers. Helping children compose simple words and sentences to express their ideas.

Print Awareness:

Familiarizing children with the concept that print has meaning (e.g., words in books, signs, or labels). Teaching directionality, such as reading from left to right and top to bottom.

Storytelling and Imagination:

Encouraging children to create and narrate their own stories. Boosting creativity while helping them understand narrative structure (beginning, middle, and end).
